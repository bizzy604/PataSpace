Act I — The Problem

House hunting in Nairobi
shouldn't feel like this

Every day, thousands of Nairobians wake up stressed, scrolling through outdated listings, paying brokers who've never stepped inside the house, and visiting properties that look nothing like the photos.

😤

The broker takes 10%

You pay a broker KES 5,000–20,000 to show you a house they've maybe visited once. They don't live there. They don't know the water schedule.

📸

Photos are lies

You travel across town, heart full of hope. The "spacious living room" is barely bigger than a closet. The photos were taken 4 years ago.

No one picks up

Landlord numbers go unanswered. Caretakers give you someone else's number. You spend weeks chasing ghosts of available houses.

"And when you finally do find a place — the outgoing tenant who knew all the real details? Already gone. Their number? Changed."
